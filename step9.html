Step 9: Test Your PC

Once you’ve completed the assembly of your PC, it’s time to power it on and test everything to make sure it works as expected.

1. Connect All Cables and Peripherals:

Ensure all necessary cables (power, display, keyboard, mouse, etc.) are securely connected to the back of the PC.

2. Turn on the PSU:

Locate the power switch on the PSU and turn it on. This will enable power to flow to your components.

3. Power on the PC:

Press the power button on your case. The PC should power up, and the lights on your components will turn on immediately. However, it may take a couple of minutes for anything to appear on the display.

4. Check for Correct Component Detection:

If the PC powers up and the correct components are displayed on the screen, then everything is functioning correctly, and you can proceed to the next step.

5. Troubleshooting:

If the PC does not power on or some components are not detected, ensure that all cables are properly connected, and double-check your connections. If the issue persists, don't hesitate to contact us for further assistance.

Now that your PC is powered on, proceed to Step 10.
