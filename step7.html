Step 7: Install the Power Supply

The power supply unit (PSU) is one of the most important components in your build, as it provides power to all your system’s components. Follow these steps to install your PSU and connect it to the motherboard and other components:

1. Install the PSU:

Begin by following the instructions provided with the computer case for installing the PSU. The PSU should be placed securely at the bottom or top of the case (depending on the case design). Ensure the fan is positioned to either intake or exhaust air correctly.

2. Connect the 24-Pin ATX Power Connector:

Locate the 24-pin ATX power connector from the PSU. This large connector is crucial for powering your motherboard. Align the connector with the 24-pin port on the motherboard and press it in firmly. The connector will only fit one way, so make sure it’s aligned properly before inserting it.

3. Connect the 8-Pin EPS Power Connector:

Find the 8-pin EPS power connector from the PSU and connect it to the corresponding 8-pin port on the motherboard, which is located near the CPU socket. Some motherboards may require a 4-pin EPS connector, so check your motherboard’s manual to confirm which one is needed.

4. Connect SATA Power Cables:

The SATA power connectors provide power to your storage devices (such as SSDs and HDDs). Locate the SATA power cables from the PSU and plug them into the small power ports on the back of each storage device.

5. Secure the PSU:

Once all cables are connected, secure the PSU in place by screwing it into the case using the screws provided with the case. Make sure the PSU is tightly secured to avoid any movement during future steps.

Now that your PSU is installed and connected, proceed to Step 8.
