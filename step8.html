Step 8: Install the Graphics Card

If you plan on using a dedicated graphics card, follow these steps to install it properly. If you don't have a GPU and will be using integrated graphics (APU), skip this step and proceed to Step 9.

1. Locate the PCIe x16 Slot:

Find the PCIe x16 slot on the motherboard, which is the long slot designed for the graphics card. It is usually located directly below the CPU socket.

2. Remove Slot Cover:

If there is a metal cover or bracket covering the PCIe slot at the back of the case, you will need to remove it. This is typically done by unscrewing or unclipping the cover from the case. Keep the screws, as you will need them later.

3. Install the Graphics Card:

Hold the graphics card by its edges to avoid touching the sensitive components. Align the connector of the graphics card with the PCIe x16 slot. Gently insert the card into the slot, pressing down firmly until it clicks into place. Ensure that the card is fully seated and that the metal bracket is flush against the back of the case.

4. Connect Power Cables:

Your graphics card may require additional power connectors. Locate the appropriate cables from your PSU and connect them to the power ports on the graphics card. These cables may be 6-pin or 8-pin connectors, depending on the model of the card.

5. Secure the Graphics Card:

Once the card is seated and connected, use the screws you removed earlier to secure the card to the case. This will ensure that it stays in place and doesn't move during operation.

Now that your graphics card is installed, proceed to Step 9.
