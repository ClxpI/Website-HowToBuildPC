Step 1: Choose Your Components

Before you start building your PC, it's essential to carefully select the components you’ll use. The choices you make here will determine the overall performance, compatibility, and cost of your computer.

Core components include the motherboard, processor (CPU), memory (RAM), storage (HDD/SSD), graphics card (GPU), and power supply unit (PSU). Additionally, you may want to include optional components like a sound card, custom cooling systems, or RGB lighting to suit your preferences.

Choosing the right components requires considering your specific needs, such as gaming, content creation, or general use, as well as your budget. Compatibility between components, such as ensuring the CPU and motherboard socket types match, is also crucial.

To simplify this process, use tools like PC Part Picker, which helps you find compatible components and build a parts list tailored to your goals.

Once you’ve chosen your components, proceed to Step 2.
