Step 6: Install the Storage

The storage drive is essential for holding your operating system, files, and programs. There are different types of drives you can install, such as NVMe M.2, SSD, or HDD. Below are the steps for installing each type.

1. Installing an NVMe M.2 Drive:

If you’re using an NVMe M.2 drive, locate the M.2 slot on your motherboard, which is typically found just below the CPU socket. Carefully insert the drive into the slot, making sure it’s properly aligned. Once it’s in place, secure the drive with the small screw provided.

2. Installing an SSD or HDD:

For an SSD or HDD, open the back of the case and locate the drive bay. Slide the drive into its designated bay and secure it with screws if necessary. Once installed, connect the SATA cable to the drive. On the motherboard, locate the SATA connector (typically located on the bottom-right side of the motherboard) and plug the other end of the cable into it.

Once your storage device is installed and connected, your computer is one step closer to being fully operational! Now, proceed to Step 7.
